Background of Fengshui

  • Fengshui in China
  • Fengshui in US

The idea of feng shui was first formally introduced in the United States in 1991 at the American Feng Shui Institute in Los Angeles, CA by Master Larry Sang. The institute evolved in order to correct common mistakes seen in various books about Feng Shui such as:
To help people discern real professionals practitioners from imposters,
To set apart actual Feng Shui from superstition
To be able to apply the fundamentals of the ancient natural science
To utilize Feng Shui correctly
For the last 15 years, classes have been held weekly, which is the only Feng Shui school to have this accomplishment.

Feng Shui’s ancient origination from China, and popularity of the idea has not yet fully reached mainstream United States. In China, Feng Shui was once banned from schools by the Chinese Communist Party as a superstition in 1949. The teaching of this idea was brought back into schools in China around 2008.

Fengshui in general

Feng Shui is a system of laws from ancient China that governs how to create a spatial arrangement and orientation in relation to the flow of energy. This system is often used to arrange rooms and create building spaces that give off a 'good energy' and creates a positive environment to be in. Having qi can affect the way you feel and think. This good energy is called ‘qi’ and is believed to be the life force of everything. Some ways in which many people believe that positive qi can be introduced include hanging tassels, having live plants, hanging wind chimes, eliminating clutter, and having an aquarium within the home. Having positive qi can bring good luck, prosperity, and happiness--which is why many people around the world still use this ancient Chinese skill today!

Research topic

Different opinions towards Fengshui

The intent of creating this survey for our topic of Fengshui was to test subjects within Truman State University with different cultural, age, and gender backgrounds and to see which categorical separation had the most impact upon the answers of the subjects. This survey gave participants the option to explain their personal experience with Feng Shui in their own words and we compiled the results of this qualitative data to come to our final conclusion.

Overall, the vast majority of our survey participants rated their knowledge of Feng Shui to be a 3 or less on a scale of 1-5 (One being little to no knowledge, and five being a lot of knowledge on the subject). Regardless of age, gender, or cultural background, a large portion of our survey’s population had little knowledge of Feng Shui. In their qualitative responses, some phrases or ideas that were often present were that of their parents exposure and how their parents used it, but rarely did people say that they, themselves, used Feng Shui in their daily lives. The only correlation that we were able to find is that those participants that responded Eastern when asked which culture were more likely to rank their knowledge as a 2 or higher on the scale of 1-5 and were also more likely to respond to the prompt about their personal experience with Feng Shui.

The reason that we believe that the results we got were accurate is because of a cultural shift that occurred that pushed back the influence of Feng Shui. In China, Feng Shui was once banned from schools by the Chinese Communist Party as a superstition in 1949. Feng Shui was not taught in schools again until around 2008. We believe that the banning of teaching within schools could be a potential reason that even the participants that said they were from Eastern cultures didn’t have as much knowledge about Feng Shui as expected. There was an entire generation of parents that went through schooling without being taught about the ancient art of Feng Shui. If this generation didn’t learn the practices from older family members, its probable to assume that they wouldn’t have taught their children much about it as well.
The United States, on the other hand, introduced the American Feng Shui Institute in Los Angeles, CA in 1991--which was the first time that Feng Shui was officially brought over the the United States. Due to this limited time of exposure, we believe that this is correlating with the results received from survey participants from Western cultures. Having this idea in the United States for only 27 years, it’s not surprising that many people don’t know a lot about Feng Shui and its positive effects.
